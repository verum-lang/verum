# Verum Community Code of Conduct

*Version 1.0 — adopted 2026-04-21*

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Verum community pledge
to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or
invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open,
welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — both as a
software project and as a forum where systems engineers and
formal-methods researchers collaborate across traditional boundaries.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all Verum community spaces,
including but not limited to:

- The **Zulip server** at `verum-lang.zulipchat.com` (all streams,
  public and private)
- All **GitHub repositories** under the Verum organisation — issues,
  pull requests, discussions, commit messages, code review comments
- The **RFC repository** and associated design-discussion PRs
- **Documentation** sites, tutorials, and examples
- **Official events**, online or in person — meetups, conferences,
  workshops, office hours
- Any **off-platform interaction** where an individual is
  representing the Verum community — public social media, blog
  posts, talks, panels, podcast appearances — when the interaction
  is identifiably about the project

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the
community in public spaces, whether or not the word "Verum" appears
explicitly.

## Our Standards

### Behaviour that helps the community

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people.
- **Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and
  experiences** — including differences in mathematical background,
  preferred proof style, or programming paradigm.
- **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.**
- **Accepting responsibility** for mistakes, apologising to those
  affected, and learning from the experience.
- **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals but
  for the overall community.
- **Assuming good faith.** When a message is ambiguous, ask for
  clarification before assuming hostile intent.
- **Citing prior work.** Verum draws on decades of research in type
  theory, category theory, compilation, and systems programming.
  Credit the papers, people, and projects whose ideas you build on.
- **Preferring substance over personality.** Critique ideas, not
  people. A technical argument is strongest when it stands
  independently of who made it.
- **Welcoming beginners.** Formal methods and systems programming
  both have steep learning curves. Patience toward newcomers is a
  core community value.

### Unacceptable behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of **sexualised language or imagery**, and sexual
  attention or advances of any kind.
- **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments**, and personal or
  political attacks.
- **Public or private harassment.**
- Publishing others' **private information** — physical or email
  address, workplace, identity, pseudonym-to-real-name linkage —
  without their explicit permission. This includes doxing and
  deadnaming.
- **Stalking**, including persistent unwanted contact across
  platforms after a clear request to stop.
- **Discriminatory jokes, slurs, or stereotypes** — regardless of
  stated intent.
- **Advocacy of violence** against individuals or groups.
- **Sustained disruption** of discussions — off-topic flooding,
  refusing to follow stream-topic norms after moderator guidance,
  brigading, sockpuppetry.
- **Bad-faith argument.** Knowingly misrepresenting others' claims,
  refusing to update in the face of evidence, or arguing for sport
  when a question was asked sincerely.
- **Credential gatekeeping.** Dismissing someone's contribution on
  the basis that they "aren't really a mathematician" or "aren't a
  real systems programmer." Contribution in this community is
  judged by the work, not the credential.
- **Academic misconduct.** Plagiarism, uncredited reuse of others'
  proofs, code, or designs, or claiming authorship of community
  work.
- **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered
  inappropriate in a professional setting.

### A note on technical disagreement

Strong technical disagreement is welcome and expected. A community
that formalises mathematics, designs compilers, and debates memory
models will see passionate debate on approaches, trade-offs, and
aesthetics. Such debate is healthy **when**:

- Participants criticise **the design, the proof, the code** —
  not the person proposing it.
- Participants engage with **the strongest version** of the
  opposing argument.
- Participants **update their position** when shown new
  information, and say so explicitly.
- Participants **acknowledge uncertainty** where it exists.

Disagreement escalating to personal attacks, sustained dismissal,
or credential-shaming crosses from "technical discourse" into a
Code of Conduct violation.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders — the core Verum team and designated moderators —
are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behaviour. They will take appropriate and fair corrective
action in response to any behaviour they deem inappropriate,
threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:

- Edit, hide, or delete comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues,
  and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of
  Conduct.
- Move messages between Zulip streams or topics when miscategorised.
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviours that
  they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
- Communicate reasons for moderation decisions where appropriate,
  respecting the privacy of reporters.

Community leaders are themselves held to this Code of Conduct and
to a higher standard of visible conduct. Abuse of moderation
privileges is itself a Code of Conduct violation.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of
Conduct, please report it. Reports are handled confidentially by
the core team.

**Primary channel**: email `conduct@verum-lang.org`.

**Zulip**: send a direct message to any core-team member whose
handle appears in the pinned message of `#meta`. If the matter
concerns a specific core-team member, escalate to a different one.

**GitHub**: for issues arising on GitHub, email is strongly
preferred over filing a public issue — a public issue may escalate
rather than de-escalate. If email is not possible, contact a
maintainer through their public profile.

### What to include in a report

A useful report usually contains:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up; kept confidential).
- Names (real, pseudonymous, or Zulip handle) of any individuals
  involved. If there are additional witnesses, please include them.
- When and where the incident occurred. Links, screenshots, or
  message IDs are very helpful.
- Your account of what happened. If there is a publicly available
  record (e.g. a Zulip thread or GitHub comment), please include
  a link.
- Any additional context.
- Whether you believe the incident is ongoing.
- Whether you would prefer a specific outcome — or whether you just
  want us to be aware.

You do not need to be certain a violation occurred in order to
report. We would rather hear about a grey-area situation early than
discover it after escalation.

### Confidentiality

We will respect the confidentiality of anyone reporting a Code of
Conduct violation. We will not publicly name a reporter without
their explicit, informed consent. We may need to share some details
with the individual being reported in order to investigate, but
will aim to protect the reporter's identity.

### Acting on reports

The core team will:

1. **Acknowledge** receipt of a report within 72 hours.
2. **Investigate**: review the relevant context, speak with the
   individuals involved, and gather any additional information.
3. **Decide** on appropriate action, informed by the Enforcement
   Guidelines below.
4. **Communicate** the outcome to the reporter and to the
   individual reported, with reasoning proportional to the
   situation.
5. **Document** the decision internally for consistency in future
   cases.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in
determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation
of this Code of Conduct. The list progresses from least to most
severe; not every violation will map cleanly to one tier.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other
behaviour deemed unprofessional or unwelcome, where the
individual was likely unaware that their behaviour crossed a line.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders
providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an
explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public
apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or
series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued
behaviour. No interaction with the people involved, including
unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct,
for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding
interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary
or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards,
including sustained inappropriate behaviour or a single egregious
act.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or
public communication with the community for a specified period.
No public or private interaction with the people involved,
including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code
of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms
may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of
community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour,
harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement
of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public
interaction within the community.

### Exceptional cases

For threats of violence, sexual harassment, stalking, or any
conduct posing an immediate safety risk to community members, the
core team may bypass the tiered progression and apply a temporary
or permanent ban immediately.

## Appeals

If you have been subject to a moderation action and believe the
decision was made in error, you may appeal by emailing
`conduct-appeals@verum-lang.org` (or contacting the core team
through the channels in `#meta`). Appeals will be reviewed by
core-team members who were not involved in the original decision.
A decision on appeal will be communicated within 30 days.

Appealing does not lift the original action during review.

## Changes to this Code of Conduct

Substantive changes to this document will be:

1. Proposed as a pull request against the main Verum repository.
2. Announced in the Zulip `#announce` stream with a 14-day
   comment window.
3. Discussed in the `#meta` stream.
4. Merged only after explicit sign-off from the core team.

Typo fixes and formatting changes do not require the announcement
window but will be logged in the commit history.
